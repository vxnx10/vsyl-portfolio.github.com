Work-study experience

During 2 years in the position of systems and networks administrator, I had to manage various missions, some very important to be resolved immediately and others less complex and undocumented.

Incident 1 – Loss of connection to display screens

Context

Several display screens no longer received real-time information.

Analysis

With the help of my supervisor, we started by checking the physical part (failing indicators, cabling). After restarting the equipment without improvement, we expanded the analysis to the operator perimeter.

Action

We accessed the operator's monitoring platform and identified a malfunction on one of its routers.

Impact

Temporary loss of display information, which could impact operational communication.

Result

After resolution on the operator side, the connection was restored and the screens functioned normally again.

Incident 2 – Non-compliant file transfer with a service provider

Context

A service provider had not received the daily files in the expected version.

Analysis

Several attempts at file transfer were made with the help of concerned colleagues in order to identify the source of the error (version, content, format).

Action

Coordination between internal teams and the service provider to restart transfers with the correct settings.

Impact

Risk of delay in data processing by the service provider.

Result

Conforming files were successfully transmitted and the situation was regularized.

Incident 3 – Slow performance on a critical company server

Context

A key server, operated 24h/24 and 7j/7, was showing slow performance impacting multiple users.

Analysis

A procedure existed verbally but was not formalised. In order to ensure the reproducibility of the intervention, I followed all the steps and documented the process.

Action

Writing a written procedure detailing the checks and actions to be taken.

Impact

Risk of slow performance of services used daily by the teams.

Result

The procedure is today used by the team, corrected and validated by my mentor.

Incident 4 – Hypervisor shutdown

Context

An alert indicated that a hypervisor hosting several virtual machines had stopped.

Analysis

Connectivity tests (ping), verification of VM failover, attempts to restart and reinstall software without success. A physical intervention was then carried out.

Action

After analysis with my mentor, the problem was identified as originating from the RAID card. The server was dismounted, the card replaced, then the hypervisor reinstalled and reintegrated into its Ovirt cluster.

Impact

Risk of unavailability of hosted services.

Result

The hypervisor was restarted without any unexpected shutdowns and successfully reintegrated into the cluster. This intervention restored the availability of the cluster without any loss of data, while improving my ability to analyse critical incidents and work in coordination with my tutor.
